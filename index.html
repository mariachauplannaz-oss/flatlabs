01 The problem

This is where your time gets wasted.

The reality

You designed the collection. You sourced the fabric. You're ready for production.

Then you end up rebuilding the same t-shirt flat that already exists in hundreds of variations — point by point, anchor by anchor. And somewhere in between, you're digging through folders trying to find the last version you swear you already made.

The pain point

Most free templates are unusable. Bad proportions. Broken groups. Random construction. They're not built for production workflows. They're built to be downloaded and abandoned.

The gap

The alternative isn't much better. Either you patch together free files that break under pressure, or you pay enterprise-level software pricing for tools built far beyond what most brands actually need.

FlatLabs defines the base layer of fashion flats — technically correct, reusable constructions for the 80% of garments that don't require custom pattern engineering. Clean, professional drawings. Ready fast, and built to be handed off when things get more complex.

Highly specialized construction and fully custom silhouettes belong with a patternmaker. FlatLabs focuses on base garment structures — the foundations most collections are built on — so technical development starts further downstream, not from scratch.

Professional flat software exists — but it's either expensive, overbuilt, or both. Free templates are the opposite problem: fast, but unreliable.

FlatLabs is the missing middle layer: technically correct flats for everyday garment construction.
